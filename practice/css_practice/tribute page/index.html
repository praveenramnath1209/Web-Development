Ken Miles

British-American Racing Driver & Engineer

Timeline of Ken Miles

  • 1918 – Born in Sutton Coldfield, Birmingham, England.
  • 1930s – Began racing motorcycles before switching to cars.
  • 1939–1945 – Served in the British Army’s tank division during World War II.
  • 1952 – Moved to the U.S. and began racing in California.
  • 1963 – Joined Carroll Shelby at Shelby American as a test driver.
  • 1966 – Won Daytona 24 Hours and Sebring 12 Hours; finished 2nd at Le Mans.
  • 1966 – Died testing the Ford J-car at Riverside International Raceway.
  • 2001 – Inducted into the Motorsports Hall of Fame of America.
  • 2019 – His story portrayed in the film Ford v Ferrari.
